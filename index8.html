Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 15, 2024 | Effective: March 22, 2024

At Nexus Browser ("Nexus", "we", "us", "our"), your privacy is our priority. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and protect your information when you use our browser, crypto wallet, VPN, and related services.

🔒 Our Commitment: Nexus Browser is built with privacy-first principles. We collect minimal data, never sell your information, and provide transparency about how your data is handled.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account Information: Email address, username, and optional profile details when creating an account
  • Wallet Information: Public wallet addresses (private keys are never transmitted to our servers)
  • Support Communications: Messages, files, and information when you contact support

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

Data TypePurposeRetention
Usage AnalyticsImprove performance and features30 days (anonymized)
Crash ReportsFix bugs and improve stability90 days
IP Address (VPN off)Security and regional complianceNot stored permanently
Device InformationCompatibility and optimization30 days

1.3 What We DO NOT Collect

  • ❌ Your private keys or seed phrases (these stay on your device)
  • ❌ Browsing history (unless you enable sync with encryption)
  • ❌ Passwords or sensitive form data
  • ❌ VPN connection logs (we operate a strict no-logs VPN)

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Provide Services: Operate and maintain Nexus Browser features
  • Improve Performance: Analyze usage to optimize speed and functionality
  • Security: Detect and prevent fraud, abuse, or security incidents
  • Communications: Send important updates, security alerts, and support messages
  • Legal Compliance: Respond to lawful requests and meet regulatory obligations

3. VPN Privacy (No-Logs Policy)

Nexus VPN operates under a strict no-logs policy. We do NOT record:

  • Your original IP address
  • Websites you visit while VPN is active
  • DNS queries
  • Connection timestamps or bandwidth usage

When VPN is enabled, your traffic is encrypted and routed through our secure servers. We only collect aggregated, anonymized metrics to ensure service quality (e.g., server load).

4. Crypto Wallet Privacy

Your crypto wallet is non-custodial, meaning:

  • Private keys are generated and stored locally on your device
  • We never have access to your funds or ability to sign transactions
  • Transaction data is broadcast to public blockchains and is publicly visible
  • Wallet addresses may be linked to your account if you choose to connect them

5. Third-Party Services & Extensions

Nexus Browser may interact with:

  • Extension Marketplace: Third-party extensions have their own privacy policies. Review permissions before installing.
  • Blockchain Networks: Transactions are recorded on public ledgers
  • Analytics Providers: We use privacy-focused analytics (Plausible, no cookies) to understand usage

6. Data Storage & Security

We implement industry-standard security measures including:

  • AES-256 encryption for data at rest
  • TLS 1.3 for data in transit
  • Regular security audits and penetration testing
  • Access controls and authentication for internal systems

Data is stored on secure servers in the United States and EU. We retain data only as long as necessary for the purposes outlined.

7. Your Rights (GDPR & CCPA)

Depending on your location, you may have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Request a copy of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate information
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data
  • Right to Portability: Receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Right to Opt-Out: Decline marketing communications

To exercise these rights, contact us at privacy@nexusbrowser.io. We respond within 30 days.

8. International Data Transfers

By using Nexus Browser, you acknowledge that your information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside your residence. We ensure adequate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) for EU-US transfers.

9. Children's Privacy

Nexus Browser is not intended for children under 18. We do not knowingly collect information from minors. If we discover such data, we will delete it immediately.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. Material changes will be notified via in-app notice or email. Continued use constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

11. Contact Us

For privacy-related inquiries, contact our Data Protection Officer (DPO):

  • Email: privacy@nexusbrowser.io
  • Mail: Nexus Labs, 123 Blockchain Ave, Suite 400, Wilmington, DE 19801, USA
